Prove It! With Asher Perlman

Asher Perlman has a dream. It involves being chased by his great aunt while his teeth fall out. Unrelatedly, he really wants to dunk a basketball. When Asher saw Ja Morant perform an insane behind the back double pump dunk during a professional basketball game, he texted a friend, the husband of his boss, and said “I think I could do that if you gave me 6 months.” Well, his boss told her boss, who happened to have a nationally broadcasted Late Night comedy show, and now Asher has 6 months to learn how to dunk, showcasing his progress every month in front of a live studio audience and millions of viewers at home. Also, Asher is a 40-year old man with a bad back, bad knees, bad elbows, and a not-great sense of his own limitations. Will Asher dunk like Morant or find out he can’t? Will he successfully posterize or painfully self-immobilize? Will he take care of business or tear his meniscus? Find out on Prove It!.

First Drafts with Evie McGee

Producer

Stephen Colbert loves his wife, Evie. That's why, every holiday, he gives her a set of thoughtfully caring and lovingly crafted greeting cards. But before he hands them over, he has to rewrite them, because they start off disgusting. In this recurring segment, Stephen and Evie go through the cards that passed muster and the ones that passed out at their step-kid’s clarinet recital. Along the way, Stephen and Evie share tender moments, fond memories, and folded cardboard that our Standards and Practices department has some notes on. First Drafts proves that if at first you don’t succeed, it’s probably because you gave your wife a card asking if she’d have a threesome with the babysitter.
